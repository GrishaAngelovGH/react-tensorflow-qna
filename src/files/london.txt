The capital city of the United Kingdom is London. 
Here are some key facts and figures about London: 
London is home to over 8.9 million people, making it the most populous city in the UK and one of the most populous cities in Europe.
The city covers an area of approximately 1,572 square kilometers (607 square miles).
London is known for its rich history, with its origins dating back to Roman times.
It has been a major settlement for over two millennia.
The city is divided into 32 boroughs, each with its own distinct character and attractions. 
London is a global financial center and is home to the London Stock Exchange, one of the largest stock exchanges in the world.
The city is a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year.
It is known for its iconic landmarks such as the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, the Houses of Parliament, and Big Ben.
London is renowned for its cultural scene, with a wealth of museums, galleries, theaters, and music venues.
The city has a diverse population, with over 300 languages spoken, making it one of the most linguistically diverse cities in the world.
London has excellent transportation infrastructure, including an extensive underground rail network known as the Tube, buses, trams, and an extensive network of cycle lanes. 
The city has a thriving culinary scene, with a wide range of international cuisines and Michelin-starred restaurants. 
London is known for its green spaces, including Hyde Park, Regent's Park, and Richmond Park, providing residents and visitors with opportunities for outdoor recreation.
The city has a temperate maritime climate, with mild winters and cool summers.
